## What it is

[oijlink](https://github.com/injoon5/oijlink) started as a Node.js URL shortener — paste a long link, get a short one. The twist I cared about was adding a light community layer on top (public links, discovery) instead of it being a private bookmark tool only.

It runs at [urlsh.kro.kr](http://urlsh.kro.kr) and uses [Primer](https://primer.style/css), GitHub's CSS framework, for styling. That was a deliberate choice: I wanted it to feel familiar to people who live in GitHub's UI, not like another generic shortener landing page.

## Stack notes

Backend is Node.js. Nothing serverless-exotic — a small VPS-style deployment with a database for links and metadata. I built it when I was still iterating on the "oij" branding (old site handles, short domains, that whole phase).

## Today

I don't touch it as often as the school projects, but it still runs. If you're looking at my GitHub and wondering where the "oij" name comes from, this era is a big part of it.
